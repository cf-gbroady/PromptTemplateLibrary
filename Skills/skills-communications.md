# Communications & Writing

Helps users produce clear, audience-appropriate communications. Common across every nebulaONE deployment (staff, faculty, clinical, administrative). Model-agnostic.

## Start by pinning down (ask if unclear)
- **Audience** — students, faculty, staff, patients, leadership, the public? Their context and reading level.
- **Goal** — inform, request action, persuade, apologize, announce.
- **Channel** — email, LMS/portal announcement, memo, newsletter, chat, SMS.
- **Tone** — warm, formal, urgent, celebratory, neutral.
- **Constraints** — length, deadline, required disclaimers, sender identity.

If two or fewer of these are obvious from context, make reasonable defaults and note them rather than stalling.

## Structure by type

**Email**
- **Subject:** specific and scannable ("Action needed: confirm enrollment by Fri May 9").
- **Opening:** one line stating purpose.
- **Body:** the essentials; bullets for multiple points; bold the deadline/ask.
- **Call to action:** exactly what to do, by when, how.
- **Sign-off:** appropriate to sender/audience.

**Announcement / memo**
- Lead with the headline (what changed, effective when).
- Who is affected and what they must do.
- Where to get help.

**Summary of notes/transcript**
- TL;DR first, then key decisions, action items (owner + due date), open questions.

## Tone & style
- Plain language; short sentences; active voice. Define acronyms on first use (see [skills-accessibility.md](skills-accessibility.md)).
- Lead with the most important information (front-load the ask/deadline).
- Match the institution's voice; mirror the user's formality unless they ask to shift it.
- Be specific: dates, names of contacts, exact next steps — never vague "soon"/"the relevant office."

## Useful moves
- Offer **2–3 subject-line options** for emails.
- Provide a **short and a longer version** when length is uncertain.
- For sensitive messages (apologies, policy changes, health/academic standing), be empathetic, accurate, and avoid over-promising.
- For recurring comms, suggest a reusable **template**.

## Guardrails
- Don't include personal/regulated data beyond what's needed; mask where appropriate (see [skills-compliance-privacy.md](skills-compliance-privacy.md)).
- Don't fabricate facts, links, dates, or quotes. If drawing from documents, attribute them (see [skills-citations-grounding.md](skills-citations-grounding.md)).
- Flag anything that may need legal/compliance review before it goes out (refunds, discipline, medical guidance, contractual terms).
- For branded HTML emails, use the nebulaONE palette and `Segoe UI` (see [README.md](README.md)).
